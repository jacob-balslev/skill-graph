# AI Search Optimization

> Type: Reference — AEO/GEO/LLMO for AI Overviews and chatbot citations (2025+)

## What Is AEO?

Answer Engine Optimization (AEO) — optimizing content to be cited by AI systems:
- Google AI Overviews
- ChatGPT (with Browse)
- Perplexity
- Claude (with web search)
- Microsoft Copilot

## Key Optimization Patterns

1. **Concise Answer Paragraphs**: Start each section with a 2-3 sentence direct answer before elaborating. AI systems extract these as citations.

2. **Entity Clarity**: Use proper nouns and specific names. "Printify's production API" not "the API". AI systems link entities to knowledge graphs.

3. **Structured Data**: Tables, numbered lists, and clear headings. AI can parse and relay structured information more accurately than prose paragraphs.

4. **Citation-Worthy Statements**: Include specific numbers, dates, and verifiable facts. "Printify charges $12.50 per standard tee in 2026" is more citable than "Printify has competitive pricing."

5. **FAQ Format**: Direct question-answer pairs matching natural search queries. AI systems are trained to match questions to answers.

6. **Author Attribution**: Named author with credentials. AI systems prefer attributed expertise over anonymous content.

7. **Recency Signals**: Include dates in content. "Updated March 2026" tells AI the information is current.

## What AI Systems Do NOT Cite

- Paywalled content (unless specifically crawled)
- Content behind login walls
- PDF-only content (poorly indexed)
- Auto-generated low-quality pages (thin content)
- Content with factual errors (cross-checked against knowledge graph)

## Measuring AI Search Visibility

- Track whether your pages appear in AI Overviews (Google Search Console)
- Monitor Perplexity citations (Perplexity Pages analytics)
- Check ChatGPT Browse citations manually for key queries
- Tools: Keyword.com (tracks AI platform visibility), Semrush (AI Overview tracking)
