# Keyword Research Methodology

> Type: Reference — the seed-to-cluster pipeline

## Phase 1: Seed Keywords (5-15 terms)

Sources:
- Your own product names and categories
- Competitor product titles and category names
- Customer language (support tickets, reviews, social media)
- Industry jargon and acronyms
- Google autocomplete suggestions for your niche

## Phase 2: Keyword Expansion (100-500 candidates)

Methods:
- Google Keyword Planner: volume and competition data
- Ahrefs/SEMrush: related keywords, questions, also-rank-for
- eRank/Marmalead (Etsy): tag suggestions, trending searches
- Helium 10 (Amazon): reverse ASIN lookup, keyword research
- Google Trends: seasonal patterns, rising queries
- Answer the Public: question-based keyword discovery
- Reddit/forums: how real people describe your niche

## Phase 3: Keyword Clustering

Group keywords by semantic similarity and shared SERP overlap.

Rules:
1. Keywords with 3+ shared URLs in top 10 results belong in the same cluster
2. Each cluster gets one primary keyword (highest volume + best intent match)
3. Secondary keywords in the cluster become supporting terms in the content
4. Assign search intent to each cluster (informational/navigational/transactional/commercial)

## Phase 4: Intent Mapping

Map each cluster to a page type:
- Informational -> Blog post, guide, tutorial
- Navigational -> Homepage, product page, feature page
- Transactional -> Product page, collection page, checkout
- Commercial Investigation -> Comparison page, review, best-of list

## Phase 5: Prioritization

Score each cluster on:
- Search volume (monthly searches)
- Competition level (keyword difficulty)
- Business value (conversion potential)
- Current ranking position (quick wins vs new opportunities)

Priority matrix:
- High volume + Low competition + High business value = Do first
- High volume + High competition = Long-term play with content strategy
- Low volume + Low competition + High conversion = Quick wins for niche products
- Low volume + High competition = Skip
